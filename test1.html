Purpose & Vision

Conversational AI for children is a fundamentally new style of play. There's no existing playbook ‚Äî no decades of research on how kids interact with AI characters, how they test boundaries, how they build (or lose) trust with a synthetic voice. The only way to build something that actually works is to build it with kids.

The Kids Lab exists because assumptions fail. What adults think children will say, how they think children will react, what they think will hold attention ‚Äî it's almost always wrong. Real children mumble, interrupt, invent words, get distracted, push limits, and surprise you. You can't design for that in a vacuum.

We answer UX questions. We collect data. And we ensure that data is annotated to reinforce our system's learning.

The Core UX Question

"What does an interactive child‚ÄìAI interaction look like, and what are the key components and obstacles from a UX and technical capabilities perspective?"

UX Research Areas

The domains we explore to answer that question

Voice UX

How children speak to AI ‚Äî tone, pacing, vocabulary, and the unique acoustic patterns of young voices in real environments.

Conversational UX

Turn-taking, interruption handling, topic shifts, and how children expect dialogue to flow versus how adults design it.

Responsible AI Design

Age-appropriate responses, safety boundaries, and building trust without manipulation or dark patterns.

Open vs. Limited Conversations

When to let children explore freely versus when to guide them within structured boundaries ‚Äî and how to transition between modes.

What We Collect

Data that trains and improves our systems across three critical domains

ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition)

Child voice data across modalities, hardware types, ages, accents, and language abilities. Off-the-shelf ASR fails with children ‚Äî we build the training data to fix that.

Safety ‚Äî Parents and Kids

Real examples of boundary-testing, sensitive disclosures, and edge cases. What children actually say when they push limits, and how parents respond.

Conversational Design

Dialogue patterns, engagement signals, confusion markers, and the moments where interactions succeed or fail.

Why This Data Matters

Every session is annotated to reinforce system learning. We don't just collect data ‚Äî we label it, categorize it, and feed it back into our models to continuously improve accuracy, safety, and engagement.

Demographic Coverage

3 countries with a large range of accents and demographic backgrounds. This isn't lab data from one school in one city ‚Äî it's real diversity.
